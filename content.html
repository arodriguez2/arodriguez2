Unemployment in the Philippines


Unemployment is an occurrence that happens when a person who is actively searching for employment is unable to find work. The problem of unemployment gives an increase in the issue of poverty. We know firsthand what poverty is being in a third world country. We see its effects every day, and we know its impact on society in general. Unemployment is often used as a means of the well-being of the economy. Not only does unemployment affect the country in general, but it also affects unemployed individuals' well- being. The most prevalent measure of unemployment is the unemployment rate, which is the number of unemployed people divided by employed people. The Philippines' unemployment rate jumped to 10 percent amid the economic downturn caused by the coronavirus crisis. A recent poll in showed that only 45% of people lost their jobs during this pandemic. That is a pretty good ratio considering that it did not even hit 100%, as said by Presidential Spokesperson Harry Roque. Even though it may not be that big of a deal, I have thought of solutions to solve unemployment.


Unemployment can result in debt and poverty, and the government has to take care of these people. Consequently, welfare spending would also increase at the same time. In that case, I came up with not just one but two solutions to cure unemployment. The first option is to reinstate slavery or, in other words, forced labor. Forced labor refers to the extraction of jobs or services from any person through violence, intimidation or threat, use of force, or coercion like deprivation of freedom and abuse of authority. Some examples of forced labor are domestic work, market trading, and prostitution. It may be involuntarily or forced work, but at least you have a job!


The other solution is to stop being lazy and start finding a job. How hard could it be? The thing is that one of the apparent reasons for unemployment is laziness. Poor people are just lazy. You can't find a job if you don't work for it. The problem with us Filipinos is that we do not have resilience even though it takes minimal effort to find a job and we continuously complain and blame the government for our shortcomings. Filipinos are all talk yet no work. The government is working hard for the country and its people, yet us Filipinos still demand our wants.


Due to unemployment affecting the government and economic status of the country, I've also come up with things the government should and should not do. First, the government should not provide better mental health support for people going through the effects of unemployment. It's all in the mind. The unemployed do not have jobs to be stressed and depressed. Second is never give training to displaced workers. Do not help the unemployed relocate to areas where jobs exist and convince prospective workers to continue or resume their education. Another thing is that they should not encourage online selling or online jobs to the unemployed, and they should not support entrepreneurs. Lastly, they should implement a tax increase for small businesses.


In conclusion, unemployment is one of the most significant difficulties facing this country and its citizens. Unemployment includes severe financial hardships and debt. Being unemployed can head to several mental health issues, particularly if an individual truly wants a job but cannot find employment. The effect of unemployment not only ends with income or financial losses but severe social and psychological problems. To solve this, Filipinos should either engage in forced labor, which will grant people jobs, although it is involuntary and forced, or Filipinos should stop being lazy and try finding a job; poor people are just lazy. With the solutions I've come up with, unemployment will be solved in no time.
